Wall Street Journal vs Pewdiepie

  •   Mon 20 March 2017
  •   Gaming
  •   Wall Street Journal vs Pewdiepie
  •   Social Media Gaming Media PewDiePie

So what is going on?

The Wall Street Journal published an article attacking Pewdiepie.

Link to Wall Street Journal

The Wall Street Journal published an article about the latest post by Youtube Gamer Pewdiepie that contained anti-semitic messages. It goes on to say that Pewdiepie has 9 videos since August that contain anti-semitic messages. Ironically you have to be a subscriber to be able to read the article.

Kjellberg (Pewdiepie) used a website called Fiverr where users can pay freelancers to perform small jobs. Kjellberg was attempting to point out how crazy the online market is where anyone can pay a person to perform anything for a small sum.

In the video, Kjellberg payed the Fiverr Funny guys (who apologized Jan 16 2017) to hold up a sign saying "Death to all Jews". In Pewdiepie's Youtube video he is seen gasping and covering his mouth. He did not think they would do it.

Kjellberg was trying to make a point. That we are at a point where the global nature of society means that any actions and services can be had for a price.

Yet I feel that there was a better way for Kjellberg to express his views instead of making anti-semitic content. Kjellberg has stated that in no way is he anti-semitic or does he support such views. But the backlash against his video content continues.

Kjellberg raised over a million dollars for charities this year. Yet non of the articles mention this or any other positice contributions he has made.

In addition one of the Wall Street Journal's journalist Ben Fritz has made his own anti-semitic comments in the past. This can be seen in GrandAUnderA's video.

GrandAUnderA <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GAmo1xGvwlw>_.

The result of the Wall Street Journal's article has been that Disney and Google have dropped Pewdiepie.

Pewdiepie is a comedian. He says some very rough and bad jokes. Many comedians do this. But I do not see the Wall Street Journal taking them on.

What is alarming for me is that the Wall Street Journal essentially bullied Pewdiepie and any companies associated with him. I do not say that what Pewdiepie did is right.

The Wall Street Journal basically had it out for Pewdiepie and labled him a neo-nazi racist based on his video. Whoa.

Basically this fubarred situation is traditional media (Wall Street Journal) vs the new media (Youtube gurus like Pewdiepie).

My views? His video was screwed up, not ethical, and bad.

My views? The Wall Street Journal is hypocritical and highly biased coverage aimed at tearing Pewdiepies reputation apart.
